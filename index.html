IEEE Aerospace and Electronic Systems Society CHAPTER

AESS IEEE stands for the Aerospace and Electronic Systems Society of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers. It is a professional society within IEEE that focuses on the advancement of theory, practice, and applications of aerospace and electronic systems. Members of AESS IEEE include engineers, scientists, and other professionals who work in these fields. The society organizes conferences, publishes journals and technical papers, and provides resources for education and professional development.

Mission and Vision

The AESS mission is to create an inclusive professional society, engaging a diverse membership globally, through technical activities. Its vision is to be essential in aerospace and electronic systems, recognized for contributions through conferences, publications, and education.

Provide a responsive and relevant professional society.

Attract, engage, aid, and retain a diverse set of members worldwide.

Accomplish through technical activities in conferences, publications, education, and member services.

Foster inclusivity and engagement through diverse technical initiatives.

Star Fest 2024

The IEEE ENIS Student Branch has collaborated with the IEEE WIE ENIS Student Affinity Group and the IEEE AESS ENIS Student Branch Chapter to organize the IEEE Star Fest. This event has been an initiative to bring students together in the glorious month of Ramadhan to enjoy each other's company in this religious month. This occasion has helped merge the usually annual Iftar coinciding with Jupiter sighting and the IEEE ENIS Student Branch 15th founding day. This in turn allowed for many students to attend both from the ENIS Student Branch and other Student Branches. The event has had great success, including a shared dinner, teambuilding activities, a stargazing/aerospace session, networking and finally the blowing of the candles, marking another year of excellece for the IEEE ENIS Student Branch.

SOLIDWORKS AND CATIA

Under the collaborative effort of PES, SCSS, RAS, AESS, CIS, and MEC, we have meticulously planned our first exciting technical workshop in this bootcamp scheduled for February 17th. This workshop will be a deep dive into the world of SolidWorks and CATIA, two prominent software solutions widely used in engineering and design. Leading the workshop Zied Kallel, whose expertise promises to offer invaluable insights into the functionalities and applications of these tools. Attendees can look forward to an immersive learning experience, discovering practical techniques and best practices for leveraging SolidWorks and CATIA effectively in their projects.

Video Editing 2024

The Computer Society IEEE ENIS SBC, in collaboration with IEEE ENIS SB and IEEE ENIS AESS Chapter, successfully conducted a workshop on Video Editing. The workshop comprised two sessions: Premiere Pro Basics: Covered the essential tools of Adobe Premiere Pro. In-depth guidance on crafting videos using Premiere Pro. Duration: 2 hours. After Effects Mastery: Explored After Effects for creating dynamic videos and models. Practical insights into advanced video editing techniques. Duration: 2 hours.

PCB Design 2024

Under the collaborative effort of PES, SCSS, RAS, AESS, CIS ENIS SBCs and MEC(materials engineering club) , we provided a workshop on PCB design technology and Altium Designer software. We covered the basics of PCBs, their historical development, advantages, and applications. Then, we explored PCB manufacturing processes and the rationale for choosing Altium Designer. Through practical demonstrations, we showcased how to use Altium Designer for schematic design, PCB layout, and routing. Participants actively engaged, asking questions and interacting with the material. By the end, they gained valuable insights and practical skills in PCB design using Altium Designer.
